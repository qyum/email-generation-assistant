# Model Comparison and Analysis

## Objective

The purpose of this evaluation was to compare two prompting strategies for an AI-powered Email Generation Assistant using the same dataset, evaluation pipeline, and custom metrics.

The evaluation was conducted using 10 unique email-generation scenarios containing different intents, facts, and tones.

The following two prompting strategies were tested:

* Model A: Basic Prompting Strategy
* Model B: Advanced Prompt Engineering Strategy

Both models were evaluated using the same custom metrics and evaluation process.

---

# Evaluation Metrics

The following three custom metrics were designed specifically for evaluating email-generation quality.

---

## 1. Fact Recall Score

### Purpose

Measures whether the generated email correctly includes the required facts from the scenario.

### Logic

* Each fact is checked against the generated email.
* Score is calculated as:

Fact Recall Score = Matched Facts / Total Facts

### Importance

This metric ensures that important business information is not omitted from the generated email.

---

## 2. Tone Accuracy Score

### Purpose

Measures whether the generated email reflects the requested tone.

### Supported Tones

* formal
* professional
* warm
* empathetic
* friendly
* respectful
* enthusiastic
* polite

### Logic

The generated email is evaluated using tone-related keywords and stylistic patterns associated with the requested tone.

### Importance

Tone alignment is important for maintaining professional and context-appropriate communication.

---

## 3. Format Adherence Score

### Purpose

Measures whether the generated email follows professional email formatting standards.

### Required Components

* Subject line
* Greeting
* Closing statement

### Logic

Each required component contributes to the final format score.

### Importance

Professional formatting improves readability and communication quality.

---

# Comparative Results

## Overall Performance

| Model   | Average Overall Score |
| ------- | --------------------- |
| Model A | 0.868                 |
| Model B | 0.801                 |

Based on the evaluation results, Model A achieved a higher average overall score compared to Model B.

---

# Metric-Level Analysis

## Fact Recall

Model A demonstrated stronger fact retention across most scenarios.

### Observation

* Model A consistently included key scenario facts.
* Model B occasionally omitted secondary details or summarized them too aggressively.

### Example Failure in Model B

Some generated emails missed details such as:

* agenda attachments
* support availability
* scheduling constraints

---

## Tone Accuracy

Both models performed reasonably well in tone consistency; however, Model A produced more stable tone alignment overall.

### Observation

* Model A generated clearer tone-specific phrasing.
* Model B occasionally produced overly generic responses despite advanced prompting instructions.

### Example

For empathetic scenarios:

* Model A included apology-focused and reassuring language.
* Model B sometimes shifted toward neutral business language.

---

## Format Adherence

Both models generally followed professional email structure.

### Observation

* Model A consistently included:

  * Subject
  * Greeting
  * Professional closing

* Model B occasionally produced formatting inconsistencies, especially in shorter responses.

---

# Biggest Failure Mode of the Lower-Performing Model

The biggest failure mode of Model B was over-constrained prompting.

### Common Issues

* Excessively rigid formatting
* Occasional omission of smaller facts
* Less natural phrasing in some responses

### Root Cause

The advanced prompting strategy introduced additional structural constraints that sometimes reduced flexibility and natural response generation.

As a result:

* some responses became repetitive
* factual completeness occasionally decreased
* outputs sometimes sounded template-based

---

# Production Recommendation

## Recommended Model: Model A

Based on the evaluation results, Model A is recommended for production deployment.

### Reasons

## 1. Higher Overall Evaluation Score

Model A achieved the highest average score across all evaluation metrics.

## 2. Better Fact Retention

Model A more consistently preserved required business details.

## 3. More Natural Email Generation

The simpler prompting approach often produced emails that sounded more natural and less rigid.

## 4. More Stable Performance

Model A demonstrated more consistent outputs across different tones and intents.

---

# Why Model A Performed Better

Although Model B used advanced prompt engineering techniques, the simpler prompting strategy used by Model A proved more effective for this task.

Possible reasons include:

* fewer prompt constraints
* greater generation flexibility
* reduced prompt overfitting
* more natural language flow

The results suggest that simpler prompts can sometimes outperform heavily engineered prompts for structured business-email generation tasks.

---

# Advanced Prompt Engineering Used in Model B

Model B incorporated several advanced prompting techniques:

## Role Prompting

The model was instructed to behave as a senior executive communications specialist.

## Few-Shot Prompting

Example emails were included to guide output behavior.

## Structured Instructions

The prompt explicitly enforced:

* subject generation
* greeting inclusion
* professional closing
* structured formatting

While these techniques improved structure, they occasionally reduced output flexibility.

---

# Conclusion

This evaluation demonstrates the importance of empirically testing prompting strategies rather than assuming that more complex prompting automatically produces better results.

Although both models generated professional business emails, Model A achieved better overall performance according to the custom evaluation metrics.

Therefore, Model A is the recommended production solution for this Email Generation Assistant project.
